Foundations Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

  • The details of neuroplasticity and growth mindset, and how they apply to me. I have lived most of my life in a fixed mindset and need to switch to a growth mindset.
  • I have learned about the difference between EQ and IQ and why/how they are both important in real-world working scenarios.
  • I have learned about Te Whare Tapa WhƒÅ, and how I can use each of the foundations to guide my habits in a healthy and productive way.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values and empathy are important for understanding, respecting, and communicating with others. Self-awareness is important for learning because learning is a self-directed activity. You need to understand yourself to understand how to learn.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

How much it is valued in the job industry and how much it can affect someone's learning and development.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Writing about myself and doing the listening and looping tasks. I don't like writing about myself, I find it difficult and uncomfortable. The listening and looping exercises were hard because 2 minutes of content was a lot to remember. I found I was able to recall the first half accurately but then forgot what the last parts were.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because human skills are needed to find a job and work successfully as a developer.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, I don't think so. I think the human skills are important real-world skills.
